SAT Math: Top 6 Tips for the No-Calculator Section

1. Know the Test to Pace Yourself

Familiarize yourself with the structure of the No-Calculator Math Test. You have 25 minutes for 20 questions, so plan to spend a little over a minute on each question.

2. Think First, Then Compute

Avoid lengthy calculations by looking for patterns and shortcuts. For example, use combination/elimination to solve systems of equations quickly.

3. Show Your Work

Write down your steps to catch mistakes and clarify your thinking, even though partial credit is not given. This is especially helpful for multi-step problems.

4. Pick Numbers

For questions with variables, choose simple numbers to simplify the problem. This technique can turn complex algebra into basic arithmetic.

5. Work Backwards

Use answer choices to solve the problem in reverse. This can be quicker and helps verify your solution, especially for multiple-choice questions.

6. Know and Practice Equation-Solving Techniques

Master key techniques like balancing equations, clearing fractions, using substitution and combination for systems, cross-multiplying, and factoring quadratics. Regular practice will make these methods second nature.
