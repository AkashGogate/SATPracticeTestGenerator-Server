6 Ways to Boost Your SAT Reading Score

Prepping for the SAT Reading Test

The SAT Reading Test accounts for 50% of your Evidence-Based Reading and Writing score. Although challenging, success lies in knowing how to approach each passage effectively.

SAT Reading Tips for a Great Score

1. Know What to Expect

You have 65 minutes to read five passages and answer 52 questions. Questions will cover vocabulary in context, main ideas, author’s purpose, comparisons, and graphical data.

2. Choose Your Own Order

Questions are in chronological order, not by difficulty. Skip hard questions and return to them later, focusing on answering as many as possible.

3. Read What You Need

Don’t read every word. Move between the passage and questions, focusing on what’s necessary to answer the questions without getting bogged down in details.

4. Leave Your Opinions at the Door

Base your answers solely on evidence from the text. Be cautious with questions using terms like infer, imply, or suggest, as the correct answer will be supported by the text.

5. Take Dual Passages One at a Time

For dual passages, answer questions about each passage individually before tackling questions that compare them. This approach prevents confusion and saves time.

6. Save Main Idea Questions for Last

Main idea questions are often best answered after you’ve tackled other questions. By then, you’ll have a better grasp of the passage’s overall themes and purpose.
