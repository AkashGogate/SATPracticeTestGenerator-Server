How to Tackle the SAT Math Calculator Section

Overview of SAT Math Calculator Section

The SAT Math calculator section has 38 questions to be answered in 55 minutes. This includes multiple choice and 8 grid-in questions. Understanding the structure helps in better time management.

Tips to Ace the SAT Math Calculator Section

1. Prioritize the Easier Questions

All SAT Math questions have equal value. Skip the tricky ones initially and focus on answering the easier questions first to maximize your score.

2. Practice Calculator Hygiene

Ensure your calculator is fully charged, clear it between problems, and keep it out of sight when not in use to avoid unnecessary issues on test day.

3. Familiarize Yourself with Calculator Functions

Learn how to use your calculator effectively, including graphing functions and solving equations. Practice using these features during your SAT prep.
